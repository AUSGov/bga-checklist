1 Decide on the type of employee

Optional

Tip - Assess your business needs

Before you start, it’s a good idea to assess your business needs so you can choose the right person for your business. Think about:

  • the tasks you need the employee to do
  • any skills and qualifications they’ll require
  • how busy the job will be
  • how long you think you’ll need someone for

Required

Decide on the employment type

Depending on the requirements of the job, you might want a:

  • full-time or part-time employee
  • fixed-term employee (non-ongoing)
  • casual employee
  • trainee or apprentice
  • contractor
  • labour-hire employee from an agency

The type of worker you decide on will affect your obligations. Find out more about the different employment types [Fair Work Ombudsman]


Required

Know the difference between an employee and contractor

To work out what your obligations will be as an employer, it’s important to know whether your worker will be an employee or a contractor

2 Assess the impacts and costs

Optional

Tip – Know what you’re in for

Consider whether you can afford the potential costs and impacts on your business, including:

  • recruitment costs
  • wages
  • allowances, overtime and penalty rates
  • tax and super
  • workers compensation insurance
  • changes to your workplace to make it accessible and safe
  • changes to the way you operate your business

Required

Find pay rates and conditions

All employees in Australia are entitled to a minimum wage. For most employees, you can find the minimum pay rates and conditions in the award that covers their industry or occupation. Check the award to find details of required:

  • pay
  • hours of work
  • rosters
  • breaks
  • allowances
  • penalty rates
  • overtime

Search for the award that applies to your business

Find the national minimum wage (if your employee isn’t covered by an award)

Check for pay rates and conditions if you’re under the WA industrial relations system


Required

Understand potential impacts on your workplace

When you take on an employee, you’re responsible for managing them and providing them with a healthy and safe working environment. To do this, you may have to make changes to your workplace. Make sure you provide:

  • safe premises
  • safe machinery and materials
  • safe systems of work
  • information, instruction, training and supervision
  • a suitable working environment and facilities

You will also need to have worker’s compensation insurance for your employee.
